# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The Procurement and Costing Sheet feature is Phase 3 of the ATA Workflow Manager. When a Project is active, the PDR (Procurement) role creates a Costing Sheet for the associated opportunity, collects quotes from up to three providers, and submits it through a two-stage approval process: first CDR (technical/scope validation), then Director/Manager (financial authority sign-off). After full approval, the PDR negotiates final pricing, generates a Purchase Order (PO), and tracks supplier ETAs against Milestone target dates. This phase closes the loop between approved training commitments (Phase 1) and active project delivery (Phase 2).

---

## Glossary

- **Costing_Sheet**: A procurement document created by the PDR for a specific Project, capturing line items with quotes from up to three providers and a selected winning quote.
- **Costing_Sheet_Line_Item**: A single row on a Costing_Sheet representing one procurable item or service, with quote fields for Provider_1, Provider_2, and Provider_3.
- **Purchase_Order**: A formal procurement document generated by the PDR after a Costing_Sheet is fully approved, referencing the approved Costing_Sheet and its selected quotes.
- **PDR**: Procurement role — creates and manages Costing Sheets and Purchase Orders.
- **CDR**: Costing role — first-stage approver for Costing Sheets (technical/scope validation). CDR approval is independent of the TRF Level 2 / Level 3 approval workflow.
- **Director_Manager**: Second-stage approver for Costing Sheets (financial authority sign-off). Any user with the Director or Manager role may act as Director_Manager approver.
- **PC**: Project Coordinator — read access to Costing Sheets and POs within their projects.
- **Provider**: A supplier or service provider from whom the PDR requests a rate or quotation. Up to three providers (Provider_1, Provider_2, Provider_3) may be quoted per line item.
- **ETA**: Estimated Time of Arrival — the date a supplier advises for delivery of a procured item or service.
- **Expected_Delivery_Date**: The delivery date derived from the linked Milestone's target date. PDR may override manually, but the system warns when the override exceeds the Milestone target date.
- **Costing_Sheet_Status**: The current state of a Costing Sheet — one of: Draft, Submitted, CDR_Approved, Negotiating, Director_Approved, Rejected.
- **PO_Status**: The current state of a Purchase Order — one of: Draft, Issued, Fulfilled, Cancelled.
- **Procurement_System**: The Procurement and Costing Sheet subsystem of the ATA Workflow Manager.
- **PO_Number_Format**: System-generated as `PO-{YYYY}-{sequential}`, e.g. `PO-2026-0001`. Sequential counter resets each calendar year.

---

## Preconditions (from Phase 2)

- A `Project` record exists with status `Active`, linked to a fully approved TRF.
- `Milestone` records exist with fields: `costing_sheet_line_item_id`, `original_cost_amount`, `original_currency` — these are populated from the Costing Sheet once it is approved.
- Roles in the system: PDR, CDR, Director_Manager, PC, Sales_User, Ops_Manager.

---

## Approval Path

Phase 3 uses **Path A** (two-stage approval):

1. PDR drafts Costing Sheet and submits
2. CDR approves (technical/scope validation) → status: `CDR_Approved`
3. PDR verifies/negotiates pricing with suppliers → status: `Negotiating`
4. Director/Manager approves (financial sign-off) → status: `Director_Approved`

Rejection at either stage returns the Costing Sheet to the PDR for remediation with the rejection reason shown. The negotiation step between CDR and Director approval allows the PDR to revise selected quotes and record negotiation history (original vs revised amounts).

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Costing Sheet Creation

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to create a Costing Sheet for an active Project, so that I can formally capture procurement line items and request quotes from suppliers.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Project has status Active, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to create one Costing Sheet linked to that Project.
2. WHEN a PDR creates a Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL set the Costing_Sheet_Status to Draft and record the creating PDR and creation timestamp.
3. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent a PDR from creating a second Costing Sheet for a Project that already has a Costing Sheet in any status. If the existing Costing Sheet is Rejected, the PDR must remediate it rather than create a new one.
4. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user who does not have the PDR role from creating a Costing Sheet.
5. IF a PDR attempts to create a Costing Sheet for a Project with status other than Active, THEN THE Procurement_System SHALL reject the action and display an error message stating that Costing Sheets can only be created for Active projects.

---

### Requirement 2: Costing Sheet Line Item Management

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to add, edit, and remove line items on a Draft Costing Sheet, so that I can capture all procurable items and their supplier quotes before submission.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Draft, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to add a Costing_Sheet_Line_Item with: description (required), unit (optional), quantity (required, positive integer), and quote fields for Provider_1, Provider_2, and Provider_3 (each comprising: provider name, quoted amount, and currency — all optional individually but amount and currency must be provided together if a provider name is entered).
2. IF a PDR submits a line item where a provider name is entered but the quoted amount or currency is missing, THEN THE Procurement_System SHALL reject the submission and display a field-level validation error indicating that amount and currency are required when a provider name is provided.
3. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Draft, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to edit any existing Costing_Sheet_Line_Item.
4. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Draft, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to delete any Costing_Sheet_Line_Item after confirmation.
5. WHEN a Costing_Sheet_Line_Item is saved, THE Procurement_System SHALL record the saving PDR and save timestamp.
6. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user who does not have the PDR role from adding, editing, or deleting Costing_Sheet_Line_Items.
7. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Submitted or Approved, THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user from modifying or deleting Costing_Sheet_Line_Items.

---

### Requirement 3: Provider Quote Selection

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to select the winning quote for each line item before submitting the Costing Sheet, so that the CDR can review which provider has been chosen and at what cost.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Draft, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to designate one of Provider_1, Provider_2, or Provider_3 as the selected provider for each Costing_Sheet_Line_Item that has at least one provider quote entered.
2. WHEN a PDR selects a provider for a line item, THE Procurement_System SHALL display the selected provider's name, quoted amount, and currency as the line item's active quote.
3. IF a PDR attempts to submit a Costing Sheet where one or more line items have no provider selected, THEN THE Procurement_System SHALL reject the submission and display a validation error listing the line items that require a provider selection.
4. THE Procurement_System SHALL display all three provider quotes side-by-side on each line item to facilitate comparison.

---

### Requirement 4: Costing Sheet Submission

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to submit a completed Costing Sheet for CDR approval, so that the procurement plan can be formally reviewed and authorised.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Draft, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to submit the Costing Sheet for approval.
2. IF a PDR attempts to submit a Costing Sheet with no line items, THEN THE Procurement_System SHALL reject the submission and display an error message stating that at least one line item is required.
3. IF a PDR attempts to submit a Costing Sheet where one or more line items have no selected provider, THEN THE Procurement_System SHALL reject the submission and display a validation error listing the affected line items.
4. WHEN a PDR submits a valid Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL update the Costing_Sheet_Status to Submitted, record the submitting PDR and submission timestamp, and send an in-app notification to all users with the CDR role.
5. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user who does not have the PDR role from submitting a Costing Sheet.

---

### Requirement 5: CDR Approval of Costing Sheet

**User Story:** As a CDR, I want to approve or reject a submitted Costing Sheet, so that I can validate the scope and procurement plan before it goes to Director/Manager for financial sign-off.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Submitted, THE Procurement_System SHALL display the Costing Sheet in the CDR's review queue.
2. WHEN a CDR approves a Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL update the Costing_Sheet_Status to CDR_Approved, record the approving CDR and approval timestamp, and send an in-app notification to the originating PDR to begin pricing negotiation.
3. WHEN a CDR rejects a Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL require the CDR to provide a rejection reason before the rejection is recorded.
4. WHEN a CDR rejects a Costing Sheet with a reason provided, THE Procurement_System SHALL update the Costing_Sheet_Status to Rejected, record the rejecting CDR, rejection reason, and rejection timestamp, and send an in-app notification to the originating PDR.
5. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user who does not have the CDR role from approving or rejecting a Costing Sheet at the CDR stage.
6. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent the same user from acting as both the submitting PDR and the approving CDR on the same Costing Sheet, even if that user holds both roles.

---

### Requirement 6: PDR Pricing Negotiation

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to verify and negotiate pricing with suppliers after CDR approval, so that I can update the selected quotes before Director/Manager final sign-off.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Costing_Sheet has status CDR_Approved, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow the originating PDR to enter a Negotiating phase by clicking "Begin Negotiation", updating the Costing_Sheet_Status to Negotiating.
2. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Negotiating, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow the PDR to revise the selected provider quote amounts on any line item.
3. WHEN a PDR revises a quote amount during negotiation, THE Procurement_System SHALL store the original CDR-approved amount as a read-only snapshot and record the revised amount, the PDR, and the revision timestamp.
4. THE Procurement_System SHALL display the original approved amount and the negotiated amount side-by-side on each line item during and after negotiation.
5. WHEN the PDR is satisfied with negotiated pricing, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow the PDR to submit the Costing Sheet for Director/Manager approval, updating the Costing_Sheet_Status to Submitted_For_Director and sending an in-app notification to all users with the Director_Manager role.
6. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user other than the originating PDR from modifying quotes during the Negotiating phase.

---

### Requirement 7: Director/Manager Approval

**User Story:** As a Director/Manager, I want to give final financial approval to a negotiated Costing Sheet, so that procurement spend is formally authorised before a Purchase Order is raised.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Submitted_For_Director, THE Procurement_System SHALL display the Costing Sheet in the Director_Manager's review queue, showing both original CDR-approved amounts and negotiated amounts side-by-side.
2. WHEN a Director_Manager approves a Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL update the Costing_Sheet_Status to Director_Approved, record the approving Director_Manager and approval timestamp, and send an in-app notification to the originating PDR.
3. WHEN a Director_Manager rejects a Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL require a rejection reason before the rejection is recorded.
4. WHEN a Director_Manager rejects a Costing Sheet with a reason provided, THE Procurement_System SHALL update the Costing_Sheet_Status to Rejected, record the rejecting Director_Manager, rejection reason, and rejection timestamp, and send an in-app notification to the originating PDR.
5. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user who does not have the Director_Manager role from approving or rejecting a Costing Sheet at the Director stage.
6. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent the same user from acting as both the CDR approver and the Director_Manager approver on the same Costing Sheet.

---

### Requirement 8: Costing Sheet Remediation

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to revise and resubmit a rejected Costing Sheet, so that I can address the reviewer's concerns and continue the procurement process.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Rejected, THE Procurement_System SHALL display a "Revise Costing Sheet" action to the originating PDR.
2. WHEN a PDR initiates revision of a rejected Costing Sheet, THE Procurement_System SHALL display the rejection reason (from CDR or Director_Manager) alongside the editable Costing Sheet form and reset the Costing_Sheet_Status to Draft.
3. WHEN a PDR resubmits a revised Costing Sheet with all validation rules satisfied, THE Procurement_System SHALL update the Costing_Sheet_Status to Submitted, record the resubmission timestamp, and notify all CDR users by in-app notification.
4. THE Procurement_System SHALL preserve the full history of all previous CDR and Director_Manager approval and rejection events on a resubmitted Costing Sheet.

---

### Requirement 9: Milestone Linkage from Approved Costing Sheet

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want approved Costing Sheet line items to populate the linked Project Milestones, so that the Phase 2 milestone records reflect the actual procurement costs.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Costing_Sheet reaches status Director_Approved, THE Procurement_System SHALL update each linked Project Milestone's `costing_sheet_line_item_id`, `original_cost_amount`, and `original_currency` fields using the corresponding approved Costing_Sheet_Line_Item's identifier, final negotiated quote amount, and currency.
2. THE Procurement_System SHALL match Costing_Sheet_Line_Items to Project Milestones using a stable line item identifier assigned at line item creation.
3. IF a Costing_Sheet_Line_Item has no corresponding Project Milestone, THE Procurement_System SHALL log the unmatched line item and SHALL NOT block the Costing Sheet approval.
4. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user from directly editing the `original_cost_amount` or `original_currency` fields on a Milestone — these fields are only writable by the Costing Sheet approval process.

---

### Requirement 10: Purchase Order Generation

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to generate a Purchase Order from a fully approved Costing Sheet, so that I can formally place orders with the selected suppliers.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Costing_Sheet has status Director_Approved, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow the PDR to generate a Purchase Order linked to that Costing Sheet.
2. WHEN a PDR generates a Purchase Order, THE Procurement_System SHALL create a PO record containing: a system-generated PO number in format `PO-{YYYY}-{sequential}` (sequential resets each calendar year), the linked Costing Sheet reference, the Project name, the PDR's name, the generation date, and one PO line item per Costing_Sheet_Line_Item showing the selected provider name, description, quantity, unit, final negotiated amount, and currency.
3. WHEN a Purchase Order is generated, THE Procurement_System SHALL set the PO_Status to Draft and record the generating PDR and generation timestamp.
4. WHEN a PO line item is created, THE Procurement_System SHALL set its Expected_Delivery_Date to the target date of the linked Project Milestone. IF the PDR overrides the Expected_Delivery_Date to a date later than the Milestone target date, THE Procurement_System SHALL display a warning indicating the delivery date exceeds the training milestone date.
5. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to mark a Purchase Order as Issued once it has been sent to the supplier, updating PO_Status to Issued and recording the issuing PDR and issue timestamp.
6. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to mark an Issued Purchase Order as Fulfilled when all items have been received, updating PO_Status to Fulfilled and recording the fulfilling PDR and fulfilment timestamp.
7. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to cancel a Purchase Order that has PO_Status of Draft or Issued, updating PO_Status to Cancelled and recording the cancelling PDR, cancellation reason, and cancellation timestamp.
8. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user who does not have the PDR role from generating or updating a Purchase Order.
9. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent more than one active (non-Cancelled) Purchase Order from being generated per Costing Sheet.

---

### Requirement 11: ETA Tracking

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to record and track supplier ETAs for each Purchase Order line item, so that I can monitor expected delivery dates and identify delays.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHILE a Purchase Order has PO_Status of Issued, THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to record a supplier-advised ETA date for each PO line item.
2. WHEN a PDR records an ETA for a PO line item, THE Procurement_System SHALL store the ETA date alongside the Expected_Delivery_Date and record the PDR and timestamp of the ETA entry.
3. THE Procurement_System SHALL display the Expected_Delivery_Date and the supplier-advised ETA date side-by-side on each PO line item so that delays are immediately visible.
4. WHEN a supplier-advised ETA date is later than the Expected_Delivery_Date for a PO line item, THE Procurement_System SHALL display a visual delay indicator on that line item.
5. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to update a previously recorded ETA date for a PO line item, recording the updated ETA, the updating PDR, and the update timestamp.
6. WHEN a PO line item's ETA is updated, THE Procurement_System SHALL retain the previous ETA value in the audit trail.
7. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow a PDR to record an actual delivery date for a PO line item when the item is received, distinct from the ETA date.
8. WHEN an actual delivery date is recorded for all PO line items, THE Procurement_System SHALL display a prompt to the PDR suggesting the Purchase Order be marked as Fulfilled.

---

### Requirement 12: Client ETA Notification (Optional — MVP flag)

**User Story:** As a PDR, I want to be reminded to notify the client when a supplier ETA changes significantly, so that the client is kept informed of delivery delays.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a PO line item's supplier-advised ETA is updated and the new ETA differs from the previous ETA by more than 7 days, THE Procurement_System SHALL log the change and display a "Notify Client Required" flag on the Purchase Order.
2. WHILE a "Notify Client Required" flag is active on a Purchase Order, THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent the PDR from transitioning the PO_Status to Fulfilled until the PDR confirms that the client has been notified.
3. WHEN a PDR confirms client notification, THE Procurement_System SHALL record the confirmation, the PDR, and the confirmation timestamp, and clear the "Notify Client Required" flag.

> **MVP Note:** This requirement is optional for the initial release. It may be deferred to Phase 6 if delivery timeline is constrained.

---

### Requirement 13: Costing Sheet and PO Read Access

**User Story:** As a PC or CDR, I want to view Costing Sheets and Purchase Orders for projects I am involved in, so that I can stay informed on procurement status without being able to modify records.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow users with the CDR role to view all Costing Sheets and their line items in read-only mode at any status.
2. THE Procurement_System SHALL allow users with the PC role to view all Costing Sheets and Purchase Orders linked to projects they are assigned to, in read-only mode.
3. THE Procurement_System SHALL display the Costing_Sheet_Status and PO_Status prominently on all list and detail views.
4. WHEN a user navigates to a Project detail page, THE Procurement_System SHALL display a summary of the linked Costing Sheet status and, if a Purchase Order exists, the PO_Status and PO number.

---

### Requirement 14: Procurement Audit Trail

**User Story:** As a manager or CDR, I want a complete audit trail for every Costing Sheet and Purchase Order, so that I can review the history of procurement decisions and ensure accountability.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Procurement_System SHALL record every Costing_Sheet_Status transition, including the actor, action, timestamp, and any associated rejection reason.
2. THE Procurement_System SHALL record every Costing_Sheet_Line_Item creation, edit, and deletion, including the actor, changed fields, and timestamp.
3. THE Procurement_System SHALL record every negotiation revision, including the original CDR-approved amount, the revised amount, the PDR, and the revision timestamp.
4. THE Procurement_System SHALL record every PO_Status transition, including the actor, action, and timestamp.
5. THE Procurement_System SHALL record every ETA entry and update on PO line items, including the previous ETA value, new ETA value, actor, and timestamp.
6. WHEN a user views a Costing Sheet or Purchase Order detail page, THE Procurement_System SHALL display the full audit trail for that record in chronological order.
7. THE Procurement_System SHALL retain all procurement audit trail records for a minimum of 7 years.
8. THE Procurement_System SHALL prevent any user from modifying or deleting an audit trail record after it has been created.
